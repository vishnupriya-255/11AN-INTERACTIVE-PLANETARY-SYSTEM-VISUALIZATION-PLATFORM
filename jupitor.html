Jupiter: The Stormy Planet

Introduction

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, a gas giant with colorful cloud bands, the Great Red Spot, faint rings, and over 95 moons. It rotates extremely fast (~10 hours) and orbits the Sun in ~12 Earth years.

Formation & Evolution

Jupiter formed early from dust and gas in the solar nebula, acquiring a massive envelope of hydrogen and helium around a likely dense core. Its strong gravity attracted most leftover gas, making it the largest planet. Rapid rotation and internal heat shaped cloud bands and storms.

Jupiter's gravity influenced the asteroid belt and orbits of other planets. Internal heat continues to drive atmospheric convection and storms like the Great Red Spot.

Key Characteristics
  • Largest planet: ~143,000 km diameter, fast rotation ~10 hrs
  • Composition: hydrogen, helium; likely dense core
  • Strongest magnetic field among planets
  • Great Red Spot: centuries-old storm with winds >400 mph
  • Faint rings of dust and debris
  • Over 95 moons; Galilean moons:
    1. Io: volcanically active, orbit 1.77 days
    2. Europa: icy surface, subsurface ocean, orbit 3.55 days
    3. Ganymede: largest moon, has magnetic field, orbit 7.16 days
    4. Callisto: heavily cratered, orbit 16.69 days
Jupiter Atmosphere

Thick hydrogen-helium atmosphere with:

  1. Colorful cloud bands from ammonia, ammonium hydrosulfide, and water clouds
  2. Powerful storms like the Great Red Spot
  3. Strong zonal winds in opposite directions at different latitudes
  4. No solid surface; gradual transition to liquid and metallic hydrogen interior
Orbits & Motion
  1. Orbital period: ~11.86 Earth years
  2. Rotation period: ~9 hours 55 minutes
  3. Axis tilt: ~3°, negligible seasons
  4. Moons’ orbits: Galilean moons regular; outer moons often retrograde
  5. Gravity influences asteroid belt and nearby planets
Key Missions
  • Juno (2016): orbiting Jupiter, studies core, magnetic field, atmosphere
  • Europa Clipper (2024): flybys of Europa to assess habitability
  • JUICE (2023): studies icy moons Ganymede, Callisto, Europa
  • Tianwen-4 (~2029 planned): Jupiter and Callisto mission
