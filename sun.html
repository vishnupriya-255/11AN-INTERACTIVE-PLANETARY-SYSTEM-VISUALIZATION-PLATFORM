Sun: Our Life-Giving Star

Introduction

The Sun is a star composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Nuclear fusion in its core releases energy as light and heat, taking ~8 minutes 50 seconds to reach Earth. This energy is essential for life on our planet.

Formation & Evolution

The Sun formed ~4.6 billion years ago from the collapse of matter in a molecular cloud. The central mass became hot and dense, starting nuclear fusion, while the remaining disk formed the Solar System.

It is now a G-type main-sequence star (G2V), converting billions of kilograms of hydrogen into energy every second. In the future, it will become a red giant, then a white dwarf, and eventually cool into a black dwarf.

Key Characteristics
  • Type: G2V Yellow Dwarf Star
  • Mass: ~1.989 x 10^30 kg (333,000 Earths)
  • Diameter: ~1.4 million km (109 times Earth)
  • Composition: ~73% Hydrogen, ~25% Helium
  • Density: ~1.4 g/cm³
  • Temperature: Core ~15 million K, Surface ~5,772 K, Corona millions of K
  • Energy Source: Nuclear fusion
  • Layers: Core, Radiative Zone, Convective Zone, Photosphere, Chromosphere, Corona
  • Rotation: ~25 days at equator, ~36 days at poles
  • Age: ~4.6 billion years
Layers of the Sun's Atmosphere
  • Photosphere: The visible surface, ~4,000–6,000°C, granulated.
  • Chromosphere: Thin reddish layer above photosphere, ~4,300°C.
  • Transition Region: Thin layer between chromosphere and corona, rapid temperature increase.
  • Corona: Outermost atmosphere, millions of degrees, source of solar wind.
Orbits & Motion
  • The Sun rotates on its axis (~25 days at equator) and orbits the Milky Way (~230 million years per galactic year).
  • Atmospheric Halos: 22° halos appear due to ice crystal refraction.
  • Dust Rings: Early solar system dust exists in faint rings tracing planetary orbits.
Key Missions
  • Parker Solar Probe (NASA): First spacecraft to fly into Sun's corona.
  • Aditya-L1 (ISRO): Sun-Earth L1 mission observing solar atmosphere.
  • Solar Orbiter (ESA/NASA): Close-up imaging of Sun and corona.
  • SDO (NASA): Maps active regions, solar flares.
  • SOHO (ESA/NASA): Studies Sun from core to solar wind.
